Martin Sayer, Assistant Men’s Tennis Coach at Virginia Tech, died unexpectedly. He was famous for his great tennis career at Radford University.

Learn about Sayer’s incredible journey from Hong Kong to world-class player, as well as his important services as a coach. His premature death has left a vacuum in the hearts of his family, colleagues, and the tennis world.

Martin Sayer, Virginia Tech’s Assistant Men’s Tennis Coach, died at the age of 36, leaving the university distraught. The university confirmed his death on Wednesday, sending the whole tennis world into shock.

Martin Sayer is remembered

Coach Martin, the Hokies’ men’s tennis coach, was hailed as an amazing person and coach with world-class tennis skill by Jim Thompson. Thompson, who spent a lot of time with Sayer, noted how much of an impact Martin made on the team and the players’ life.

Sayer’s adventure began in Hong Kong, where he demonstrated great tennis talent. His abilities drew the attention of Radford University coach Mike Anderson, who offered him a full scholarship. Sayer made history at Radford by winning a school-record 114 singles matches and became the program’s first player to attain a national ranking.

A Promising Coaching Career

Sayer turned into teaching after his great playing career, where he continued to encourage future players. While obtaining his MBA, he worked as an assistant for the Radford men’s team. Later, he became an instructor at the Smith Stearns Tennis Academy in South Carolina.

Sayer joined the Virginia Tech women’s tennis team as an assistant coach in 2012. He then returned to Radford as the head women’s tennis coach in 2014, citing his love for both the university and the sport.

A Family Legacy

Martin Sayer’s legacy extends beyond his tennis accomplishments. His wife, their 5-year-old kid, and his siblings, Nicholas and Natalie, both of whom played for Radford University, survive him. His death has left a vacuum in the lives of his loved ones and colleagues, who remember him not only as a great athlete but also as a great guy.

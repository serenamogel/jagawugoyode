Who is Nick Hungerford wife, Nancy Hungerford?

Who is Nick Hungerford’s wife? Well, our checks reveal she is called Nancy Hungerford. Following the devastating news that the millionaire has just a few days to die, netizens want to know their role of his as he prepares for his gloomy journey ahead.

Nick Hungerford is a British millionaire and entrepreneur who is dying from terminal bone cancer with as little as two months to live and has set up a charity for children bereaved by an incurable disease.

Nick is the Founder of Nutmeg. He founded Nutmeg after quitting his job as a stockbroker due to his frustration with the industry’s exclusivity and lack of openness.

He made Nutmeg a unicorn in the fintech industry. Nick has an MBA from Stanford University and previously worked at Barclay’s and Brewin Dolphin before starting Nutmeg. He gives lectures at the London Business School, Stanford, and Harvard.

What happened to Nick Hungerford?

The 43-year-old co-founder of the online wealth management company Nutmeg, Nick Hungerford, is in excruciating discomfort due to 30 tumours spread across his body caused by Ewing sarcoma. The married man, whose daughter Elizabeth is two years old, underwent frequent chemotherapy or radiotherapy, which increased his life expectancy by 18 months.

And Mr Hungerford, a native of the West Country who now resides in Washington, DC, has established the charity Elizabeth’s Smile in her honour to aid young people who have lost loved ones.

The entrepreneur, whose business was sold to JP Morgan in June 2021 for about £700 million, stated that he wanted the charity to offer resources for children in the form of grief manuals created by professionals and to offer a safe online community.

Who is Nick Hungerford wife, Nancy Hungerford?

Mr Hungerford’s wife is Nancy Hungerford. Nancy is his co-partner in running the charity. Nancy happens to be a former news anchor, reporter and producer for CNBC International in Europe and Asia.

In a heartbreaking interview with the Daily Telegraph, Nick shed light on his predicament saying: ‘It’s not the pain or fear of death that worries me most, it’s leaving my wife and my toddler daughter.

‘Elizabeth is just two and a half, and she will have to grow up without me. She is already a true Daddy’s girl – we share incredible hugs, she misses me when I’m at the hospital, and greets me with her toy stethoscope, saying how brave I am.

‘The thought of missing her first day of school, of not giving a speech at her wedding, buries me with emotion.’

Mr Hungerford added that Elizabeth is ‘too young to have active, internal memories of me’, but he has done everything possible ‘to show how much I love her’.

He has written letters and messages to be given to her in the future and even used an artificial intelligence website to video himself answering hundreds of personal questions, so she can ‘talk’ to him online in the future.

Kathleen Denise Quinlan Abbott is an American actress who has been working in both the film and television industries. She is mainly known for her heart-winning performances given in movies and television series. Katheleen is an award-winning actress as she has been treating her fans and the audience with her excellent acting prowess.

She is notable for winning a Golden Globe nomination for her performance in the 1977 film of the novel I Never Promised You a Rose Garden.

Career

Katheleen has acted in many feature films, short films, and television series in almost 50 decades of her career.

As a teenager, Quinlan appeared in uncredited film titled ‘One Is a Lonely Number’, released in 1972. Later, she debuted with a credited role in George Lucas’ 1973 film American Graffiti.

She then made appearances on the television in guest roles during the 1970s included Police Woman, Kojak, Ironside, Emergency! and The Waltons.

Among most popular roles, Deborah, a sixteen-year-old schizophrenic, in the film version of the novel I Never Promised You a Rose Garden, for which she earned a Best Actress in a Motion Picture-Drama Golden Globe nomination, and others are prominent.

In addition to this, she became famous for starring as Marilyn Lovell, the wife of Tom Hanks’s astronaut character Jim Lovell, in the 1995 movie Apollo 13, for which she earned both a Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture Golden Globe nomination and a Best Supporting Actress Academy Award nomination.

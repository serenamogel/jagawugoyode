What district does Adam Kinzinger represent?

Adam Kinzinger is an American politician serving as the U.S. representative for Illinois’s 16th congressional district.

Adam Daniel Kinzinger was born on February 27, 1978, in Kankakee, Illinois, United States. His father Rus Kinzinger is the CEO of faith-based organizations, while his mother  Betty Jo Kinzinger worked as an elementary school teacher. He graduated from Normal Community West High School in 1996.

Adam Kinzinger earned a bachelor’s degree in political science from Illinois State University in 2000. He served as an intern for former U.S. Senator Peter Fitzgerald following his graduation from Illinois State. He served on the McLean County Board but resigned in 2003 to join the United States Air Force. He is a lieutenant colonel in the Air National Guard as of now.

Adam Kinzinger is a member of the Republican Party and was first elected to Congress in 2010 from the 11th district. He was ranked 5th on The Hill’s 2011 annual “50 Most Beautiful People” list. He married Sofia Boza-Holman, a former aide to John Boehner and aide to Vice President Mike Pence on February 16, 2020. Together, they have a son, Christian Adam Kinzinger, who was born in January 2022.

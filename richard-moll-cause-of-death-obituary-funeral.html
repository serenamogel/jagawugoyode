Richard Moll cause of death, obituary, funeral

Find information about Richard Moll’s cause of death, obituary, and funeral in this report. Richard Moll was an American actor known for playing Aristotle Nostradamus “Bull” Shannon, a bailiff, on the NBC sitcom Night Court from 1984 to 1992. Richard Moll, born on January 13, 1943, in Pasadena, is currently an octogenarian. He obtained his education from UC Berkeley and embarked on a career in acting, commencing at the esteemed Will Geer Theatre, where he showcased his talent in various Shakespearean productions.

Richard Moll biography and career

Charles Moll, credited as Moll, portrayed Joseph Smith in the 1977 film Brigham. Due to his height and deep voice, Moll often played hulking or imposing characters. In 1979, he played Eugene, a gangster, in the Happy Days episode “Fonzie’s Funeral”. Moll co-starred with Jan-Michael Vincent and Kim Basinger in the 1981 film Hard Country and played the abominable snowman in the comedy feature film Caveman.

He also had a small part in the Mork & Mindy episode “Alienation” and played the sorcerer Xusia in The Sword and the Sorcerer in the same year. In 1983, Moll shaved his head for the role of Hurok in the science fiction B movie Metalstorm: The Destruction of Jared-Syn.

The producers of Night Court liked the look so much that they asked him to keep it for his audition. Moll also used the Bull persona in commercials for Washington’s Lottery. Moll played Big Ben in the 1986 horror film House and earned a Saturn Award nomination for the role. He made guest appearances on Highlander: The Series, Babylon 5, Married… with Children, and Hercules: The Legendary Journeys, playing various characters such as a villain, a lurker criminal, and a cyclops.

Richard Moll death: How did he die?
